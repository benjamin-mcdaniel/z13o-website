Meatable - satisfy the world’s appetite for meat without harming animals.

The Author May 26, 2022 Startups

Meatable is a Dutch Food production company based in Delft, Zuid-Holland, The Netherlands. It was founded in the year, 2018 and the legal name for the company is Meatable B.V. Mark Korter is the founder and CEO of the company. Krijn De Nood guides the company's strategic direction whereas, Daan Luining is the co-founder and CTO of Meatable. It has 15 investors including Possible Ventures, which backs ambitious teams building frontier technology to positively impact people and the planet, and BlueYard which seeks to invest in projects that aim to decentralize markets. Meatable has raised a total of $172.8M in funding over 8 rounds. Their latest funding was raised on Mar 23, 2021, from a Series A round.

Some people love to enjoy meat whereas others think that it is very cruel to slaughter animals just for the sake of a hamburger. Moreover, It is believed that beef production causes greenhouse gas emissions through the agricultural production process and land-use change. One should keep in mind that Greenhouse gases are responsible for global warming which could deteriorate the planet entirely. To address these issues Meatable came into existence. The Company produces real and guilt-free meat, which is made from cell linings of original animals which means one hasn't had to kill the animals for the sake of meat. The guilt-free meat is the same as the real meat in texture as well as taste. The company aims to appease the world's longing for meat without maltreating people and animals. It is pioneering in the production of meat without any harm and drawbacks. It ensures efficient, sustainable, and harm-free meat.

The company doesn't keep any secrets but rather shares the real science behind its product. Firstly the sample is taken from an unharmed pig or cow and then replicates the natural process of fat and muscle and mixes two elements to produce meat. This whole process takes a couple of weeks instead of waiting for a cow around 2-to 3 years to develop enough meat to get it slaughtered. No antibiotics are used during the process. Trees are saved which are otherwise used for the grazing of animals leading to deforestation. To produce 1 pound of beef, 1799 gallons of water are used. Also, the average western diet comprises over 1000 animals in a lifetime but thanks to the company no slaughtering are done. The company is doing its best so far to satisfy its customers.
